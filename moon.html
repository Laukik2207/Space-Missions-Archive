MOON

Humanity’s first destination beyond Earth. A symbol of ambition, exploration, and the beginning of space travel.

Historic Moon Missions

Apollo 11 (1969)

First successful human landing on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin made history.

Apollo 17 (1972)

The last crewed mission to the Moon during the Apollo program.

Chandrayaan-3 (2023)

India’s historic landing near the Moon’s south pole.

Artemis I (2022)

NASA’s return-to-Moon program preparing for future human exploration.

Moon Exploration Timeline

1959

Luna 2 becomes the first human-made object to reach the Moon, marking the beginning of direct lunar exploration.

1966

Luna 9 achieves the first successful soft landing on the Moon and transmits the first surface images back to Earth.

1968

Apollo 8 becomes the first crewed mission to orbit the Moon, delivering the iconic "Earthrise" photograph.

1969

Apollo 11 achieves the first human Moon landing. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walk on the lunar surface.

1972

Apollo 17 becomes the final crewed Moon landing mission of the Apollo program.

1994

Clementine mission maps the lunar surface in detail, providing new scientific insights.

2008

Chandrayaan-1 confirms the presence of water molecules on the Moon’s surface.

2013

China’s Chang’e 3 lands successfully, marking China’s first soft lunar landing.

2019

Chang’e 4 achieves the first-ever landing on the far side of the Moon.

2022

Artemis I completes an uncrewed mission around the Moon, paving the way for future human return missions.

2023

Chandrayaan-3 successfully lands near the Moon’s south pole, opening a new era of polar exploration.
